\begin{block}{Motivation: $\boldsymbol{^{85}}$Kr Background}
  $\boldsymbol{^{85}}$Kr, which is from the nuclear power plant and nuclear weapons testing, contributes to the electronic recoil background of the dark matter search experiment by $\boldsymbol{\beta}$ decay with a Q-value 687.0~keV~\cite{XENON:2022ltv, LZ:2025zpw, PandaX:2024jjs}.
  \vspace{8pt}
  \begin{center}
    \includegraphics[width=0.51\linewidth]{bkg_only_full_energy.pdf}\hfill
    \includegraphics[width=0.47\linewidth, trim=0 0 0 14.3cm, clip]{DataPlot_bgfit.pdf} \\[4pt]
    \captionof{figure}{\small $\boldsymbol{^{85}}$Kr background in XENONnT~\cite{XENON:2022ltv}~(left) and LZ~\cite{LZ:2025zpw}~(right) experiments for electronic recoil new physics search.}
  \end{center}
  \vspace{8pt}
  The abundance of $\boldsymbol{^{85}}$Kr in $^{\mathrm{nat}}$Kr is taken as $\boldsymbol{2\times10^{-11}}$ based on the seasonal measurements of $\boldsymbol{^{85}}$Kr near XENONnT experiment~\cite{XENON:2022ltv}. The $^{\mathrm{nat}}$Kr concentration in XENONnT was measured to be (56 $\pm$ 36)~parts-per-quadrillion (ppq)~\cite{XENON:2022ltv} using the rare gas mass spectrometry (RGMS) method, which is the most sensitive method to date. However, the large uncertainty of this measurement motivates the development of a new method with better sensitivity and precision.
\end{block}
\vspace{1.0cm}

\begin{block}{Methodology: Freeze out Xenon}
  We propose to measure the $^{\mathrm{nat}}$Kr concentration by Residual Gas Analyzer~(RGA). The xenon in the gaseous mixture has to be frozen out to avoid saturating the RGA. The freeze-out process is achieved by a cold trap, which is cooled below 161.4~K by liquid nitrogen~(LN${_2}$) or cryocooler following the approach of Ref.~\cite{Silk:2023hlt}. The $^{\mathrm{nat}}$Kr can pass through the cold trap and be measured by the RGA, while the xenon is frozen and trapped in the cold trap.
  \vspace{8pt}
  \begin{center}
    \includegraphics[width=0.98\linewidth]{rga_cold_trap-modified 2023 thesis.drawio.pdf} \\[4pt]
    \captionof{figure}{\small Piping and instrumentation diagram of XTRACT.}
  \end{center}
  \vspace{8pt}
  The operation procedure during measurement is as follows:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Collect the gaseous mixture containing xenon and krypton into the source bottle.
    \item Cool down the cold trap with liquid nitrogen or a cryocooler.
    \item Use mass flow controller to control the flow of the gaseous mixture into the cold trap, where xenon is frozen out and Kr passes through.
    \item Regulate impedance valve to control the total pressure near RGA to be low enough.
    \item Measure the $^{\mathrm{nat}}$Kr partial pressure versus time by RGA.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{8pt}
  The total amount of gas used is $\boldsymbol{\mathcal{O}}$(100)~g. The loss of xenon during each measurement is estimated to be less than 1~g, which is negligible compared to the total xenon inventory of the experiment (several tons).
\end{block}
\vspace{1.0cm}

\begin{block}{System Design: In-Situ Leak-Tight Welded System}
  The system is designed with the following key features to ensure reliability and sensitivity:
  \vspace{8pt}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item In-situ auxiliary system directly coupled to the main volume.
    \item Welded connections throughout the internal gas path minimize potential leak paths.
    \item A Swagelok VCR\textsuperscript{\textregistered} fitting is used only at the main-volume interface for a removable leak-tight connection.
    \item The system is tested using LN${_2}$ and cryocooler. Eventually LN$_2$-free operation reduces cryogenic handling complexity.
\end{itemize}
\end{block}

\begin{alertblock}{Conclusions \& Outlook}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item[$\checkmark$] We have designed and assembled a leak-tight system for measuring the $^{\mathrm{nat}}$Kr concentration in xenon with ppq sensitivity.
    \item[$\checkmark$] The system is currently being commissioned and tested with LN${_2}$ and cryocooler.
    \item[$\rightarrow$] We expect to reach a sensitivity of $\mathcal{O}$(100)~ppq with $\mathcal{O}$(100)~g xenon, which will provide a more precise measurement of the $^{\mathrm{nat}}$Kr concentration and help reduce the uncertainty of the $\boldsymbol{^{85}}$Kr background in dark matter search experiments.
  \end{itemize}
\end{alertblock}
